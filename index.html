About Me

I am a researcher working in the field of geospatial artificial intelligence, remote sensing, and natural hazard analysis. My work focuses on landslide detection, disaster information extraction from social media, and multi-source geospatial data fusion for improving situational awareness.

I am particularly interested in integrating satellite imagery, environmental data, and text-based information (e.g., tweets) to infer where and when landslides occur.

Research Interests

  • Landslide detection and mapping using optical & SAR imagery
  • Disaster information extraction from social media
  • Geospatial data fusion (DEM, rainfall, landcover, susceptibility)
  • Knowledge graphs and hypergraphs for natural hazards
  • Deep learning for GIScience

Selected Projects

  • Multi-source Satellite Landslide Detection
    Combining Sentinel-1 SAR and Sentinel-2 optical imagery for robust landslide mapping.
  • Social Media–based Landslide Location Inference
    Integrating tweets, DEM, rainfall, susceptibility maps to infer event location and time.
  • Landslide Knowledge Representation
    Applying hypergraph theory to model complex mechanistic relationships.
